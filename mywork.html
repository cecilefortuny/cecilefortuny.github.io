~ Research Experience ~

I am molecular biologist with an emphasis on viral engineering.
My academic background is in biotechnology, molecular biology, pharmacology, drug and vision science.

Gene Therapy for Inherited Retinal Degeneration
PhD Candidate in John Flannery's Lab - Vision Science Graduate Program (2013-present)
- Development of a mutation-independent combination therapy for the treatment of inherited retinal degenerations such as retinitis pigmentosa (RP).
- Elucidating the structural changes and molecular mechanisms underlying 5’UTR mutations in a model of retinal degeneration
- Characterization of CRB1 gene therapy in a mouse model of Leber's Congenitcal Amaurosis.
Rotation in Marla Feller's Lab - Vision Science Graduate Program (2013-2014)
- Impact of Müller glia cell maturation on spontaneous activity in the developing retina.
- Role of Starbust amacrine cells (SACs) in the generation and adaptation of direction selectivity.
Gene Therapy for Inherited Retinal Degeneration
Visiting Scholar & Junior Specialist - Hellen Wills Neurosciences Institute at UC Berkeley (Jan-Aug 2012)
- Engineering AAV variants for intravitreally transducing bipolar and retinal pigment epithelium cells in the retina.
- AAV-mediated delivery of retinoschisin for the treatment of X-linked Retinoshisis (XLRS) in a degenerative mouse model.
Endothelial Function and Vascular Protection Research
Master’s internship in Valérie Schini Kerth's lab, Faculty of Pharmacy, Strasbourg (Fall 2011)
- Investigated the role of apoptotic and epigenetic integrator protein UHRF1 in the activation of the p73-dependent pro-apoptotic pathway in an acute lymphoblastic leukemia cell line.
Endothelial Function and Vascular Protection Research
Undergraduate Researcher at the Europeen Center of Diabete Research (CEED), Strasbourg (Summer 2009)
- Studies liver regeneration and the influence of the diabetic environment.
